## What is my IP?

Your public IP address is the address websites can see when your browser connects to them. It can reveal approximate location, ISP, ASN, organization, or network type.

## What is a DNS leak?

A DNS leak happens when DNS requests are sent outside the expected proxy or VPN path. This may reveal resolver or network details that do not match your visible public IP.

## Is a VPN always safer?

Not automatically. A VPN can change your public IP address, but DNS, WebRTC, browser language, time zone, or IP reputation can still create visible inconsistencies. Run a full check instead of relying on one signal.

## Should I test again after changing settings?

Yes. Re-test after changing proxy, VPN, DNS, browser profile, browser extensions, operating system, device, or network connection.
